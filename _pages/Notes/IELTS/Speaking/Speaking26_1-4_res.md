# Speaking2026年1-4月保留题
## Part1
### 19、Plants
#### Do you keep plants at home?
> Yeah, I do. I keep a few small plants at home. Nothing fancy. Just easy ones. They make my room feel fresher. And watering them is kind of relaxing.
有的。我家里养了几盆小植物。不是什么很高级的。就是那种好养的。会让房间更清爽。浇水也挺解压的。

>Not really. I’d like to, but I’m not good at taking care of plants. I forget to water them. And then they die. So I just keep my place simple.
不太养。我其实想养，但我不太会照顾植物。我经常忘记浇水。然后它们就挂了。所以我干脆让家里简单点。

#### What plant did you grow when you were young?
>When I was young, I grew green onions. It was super easy. I just put the roots in water. Then it started growing again. I felt proud, even though it was a tiny plant.
我小时候种过小葱。特别简单。把葱根泡在水里就行。过几天它就又长出来了。虽然很小，但我当时挺有成就感的。

>Not really. I didn’t grow plants much as a kid. I am living in an apartment. We didn’t have a garden. So I only saw plants in parks or at school.
不太种。我小时候基本没怎么种植物。我住公寓。也没有院子。所以我更多是在公园或学校里看到植物。

#### Do you know anything about growing a plant?
>Yeah, a little. First, you need enough sunlight. Then you need to water it, but not too much. Good soil matters too. And you should check it often. If the leaves turn yellow, something is wrong.
知道一点。首先要有足够的阳光。然后要浇水，但别浇太多。土也很重要。还要经常看看状态。如果叶子发黄，基本就是哪里出问题了。

>Not much, to be honest. I only know the basics. I usually just follow the instructions online. If it looks dry, I water it. That’s pretty much it.
说实话不太懂。我就知道一些基础的。我一般就是照着网上的说明来。看起来干了就浇水。差不多就这样。

#### Do Chinese people send plants as gifts?
>Yeah, sometimes. People may send plants like orchids or lucky bamboo. It looks nice. And it feels meaningful. It can stand for good luck and a fresh start. It’s also a safe gift for a new home.
会的，有时候会送。比如兰花、富贵竹这种。好看，也挺有寓意。可以代表好运和新的开始。送给搬新家的人也比较稳。

>Not that often, I think. Most people still prefer practical gifts. Like fruit, snacks, or red envelopes. Plants are nice, but they need care. Some people worry the plant might die. So they avoid it.
我感觉不算特别常见。大多数人还是更爱送实用的，比如水果、零食、红包。植物当然好看，但需要照顾。有些人会担心养死了不吉利，所以会避开。

### 20、Public places
#### Have you ever talked with someone you don't know in public places?
>Yes, I have. For example, once I was waiting for the bus and an old lady asked me about the bus route. We ended up chatting for a few minutes about the traffic in the city. It was quite a warm moment in an ordinary day.
有过。比如有一次我等公交的时候，一位老太太问我路线，我们最后还聊了几分钟关于城市交通的话题。那感觉挺温暖的，就发生在普通的一天里。

>Not really. I’m a bit shy with strangers, so I usually just keep to myself in public. If someone starts a conversation, I’ll answer politely, but I rarely take the initiative to talk first.
没有吧。我和陌生人有点害羞，所以在公共场合一般都是自己待着。如果有人跟我搭话，我会礼貌回答，但我很少主动去聊。

#### Do you wear headphones in public places?
>Yes. I usually put on my headphones when I’m on the subway or walking alone. Listening to music or podcasts makes the time pass faster, and it also helps me block out the noise around me.
是的，我会。在地铁上或者独自走路的时候，我通常会戴上耳机。听音乐或播客能让时间过得更快，也能帮我隔绝周围的噪音。

>No. I don’t wear headphones that often in public, because I’m worried I might miss important announcements, like on the bus or at the train station. Sometimes I just prefer to stay aware of what’s happening around me.
不太会。我在公共场合不怎么戴耳机，因为我担心会错过重要的广播通知，比如公交车或火车站里的提醒。有时候我更喜欢保持对周围情况的注意。

#### Would you like to see more public places near where you live?
>Definitely. I’d love to see more public places like parks or sports grounds near my home. They would give people more space to exercise, relax, and spend time with family or friends, which can really improve the quality of life.
当然想。我很希望在我家附近能有更多的公共场所，比如公园或运动场。这样人们就有更多地方锻炼、放松，和家人朋友一起度过时光，这确实能提升生活质量。

>Actually, not really. There are already several public places near where I live, like a big park and a library. I think that’s enough, because if there are too many, they might even cause more noise.
其实不用太多。我家附近已经有好几个公共场所，比如一个大公园和一个图书馆。我觉得这已经够了，如果太多，可能还会带来更多的噪音。

#### Do you often go to public places with your friends?
>Yes, quite often. I usually hang out with my friends in public places like parks, shopping malls, or cafés. It’s a nice way to relax and catch up after a busy week.
是的，经常会去。我通常会和朋友去一些公共场所，比如公园、商场或者咖啡馆。这是放松和在忙碌一周后聊聊近况的好方法。

>Not really. I don’t often go to public places with friends, because we usually just meet at someone’s home or chat online. It feels more comfortable and private that way.
不太会。我不常和朋友去公共场所，因为我们通常会在谁家见面，或者直接网上聊天。那样更舒服，也更私密。

### 21、Rules

#### Are there any rules for students at your school?
>Yes, there are. For example, students are not allowed to use phones in class, and we have to wear our student ID on campus. These rules are not too strict, but they help keep order at school.
有的。比如学生上课不能用手机，而且在校园里必须佩戴学生证。这些规定不算太严格，但能帮助维持学校的秩序。

>Not really. To be honest, there aren’t that many strict rules at my school. As long as we attend classes on time and finish assignments, teachers usually don’t interfere much with our daily life.
没有太多。老实说，我们学校严格的规定不多。只要按时上课、完成作业，老师一般不会太干涉我们的日常生活。

#### Do you think students would benefit more from more rules?
>Yes, I think so. More rules can help students stay disciplined and focused. For example, if schools set stricter rules about homework and class attendance, students are less likely to slack off and can develop better study habits.
我觉得会。更多的规定能让学生更有纪律，更专注。比如如果学校在作业和出勤上设更严格的规定，学生就不太会偷懒，也能养成更好的学习习惯。

>No. I don’t think more rules always mean more benefits. Too many restrictions might make students feel stressed or even rebellious. It’s better to give them some freedom, so they can learn how to manage themselves.
不一定。我不觉得规定越多就越有好处。太多限制可能会让学生感到压力，甚至产生逆反心理。还是给他们一些自由更好，这样他们才能学会自我管理。

#### Have you ever had a really dedicated teacher?
>Yes. In high school, my English teacher was really dedicated. She often stayed after class to answer our questions and even prepared extra materials for us. Her passion for teaching really inspired me.
有的。在高中时，我的英语老师非常敬业。她经常在课后留下来解答问题，甚至还为我们准备额外的资料。她对教学的热情真的很鼓舞我。

>Not really. Most of my teachers did their jobs properly, but I wouldn’t say any of them were extremely dedicated. They just followed the normal routine, without putting in much extra effort.
没有特别有。我大多数老师都尽职尽责，但我不觉得他们特别敬业。他们基本都是按照正常流程来，并没有投入太多额外精力。

#### Do you prefer to have more or fewer rules at school?
>Actually, I’d prefer more rules at school. Clear rules can keep order and make sure everyone behaves properly. For example, if there are strict rules about attendance, students are less likely to skip classes.
其实我更喜欢学校有多一些规定。明确的规矩能维持秩序，确保大家都表现得当。比如如果有严格的出勤规定，学生就不太可能逃课。

>Honestly, I’d rather have fewer rules. Too many restrictions can make students feel stressed or even rebellious. With more freedom, students can learn how to manage themselves, which is also an important skill.
说实话，我更喜欢少一些规矩。太多限制会让学生感到压力，甚至产生逆反心理。如果能有更多自由，学生也能学会自我管理，这也是一项重要的能力。

#### Have you ever had a really strict teacher?
>Yes, I have. In middle school, my math teacher was very strict about homework and discipline in class. At first I felt nervous, but later I realized her strict attitude actually pushed me to study harder.
有过。在初中的时候，我的数学老师对作业和课堂纪律非常严格。一开始我感到紧张，但后来我意识到她的严格态度确实让我更加努力学习。

>No, I haven’t really had a very strict teacher. Most of my teachers were quite reasonable, and they focused more on encouraging us rather than punishing us. I think that made the classroom atmosphere more relaxed.
没有，我并没有遇到过特别严格的老师。我的老师大多都很通情达理，他们更注重鼓励我们，而不是惩罚我们。我觉得这让课堂氛围更加轻松。

#### Would you like to work as a teacher in a rule-free school?
>No, I wouldn’t. Without rules, it would be really hard to manage the classroom, and students might not take their studies seriously. I think some structure is necessary to keep order and make teaching effective.
不会。我不愿意在没有规则的学校当老师。没有规矩的话，课堂很难管理，学生可能也不会认真学习。我认为一定的制度是必要的，才能维持秩序，让教学有效。

>Yes, I’d like to give it a try. A rule-free school might encourage more creativity and independence in students. It would be challenging, but I think it could also be exciting to teach in a freer environment.
会的，我愿意尝试。在没有规则的学校里，学生可能会更有创造力和独立性。虽然会有挑战，但我觉得在更自由的环境里教学也会很有趣。

### 22、Shoes

#### Do you like buying shoes? How often?
>Definitely. I enjoy buying shoes, especially sneakers. I usually get a new pair every three or four months, because I like to match them with different outfits and follow fashion trends.
当然。我挺喜欢买鞋的，尤其是运动鞋。基本上三四个月就会买一双新的，因为我喜欢和不同的衣服搭配，也想跟上潮流。

>Honestly, not much. I don’t buy shoes very often, maybe only once a year. As long as the ones I have are comfortable and durable, I don’t feel the need to get new ones.
说实话，不太喜欢。我不怎么经常买鞋，大概一年才买一次。只要我现有的鞋子舒服、耐穿，我就没有换新的必要。

#### Have you ever bought shoes online?
>Absolutely. I’ve bought shoes online several times. It’s super convenient because I can compare prices and styles without going from store to store. The only drawback is that sometimes the size doesn’t fit perfectly.
当然有。我在网上买过好几次鞋。非常方便，因为不用一家家逛店就能比价格、挑款式。唯一的缺点就是有时候尺码不太合适。

>Actually, no. I haven’t bought shoes online, because I prefer trying them on first. Comfort matters a lot to me, and I don’t trust the photos or size charts on shopping websites.
其实没有。我没在网上买过鞋，因为我更喜欢先试穿一下。舒适度对我来说很重要，而我不太相信购物网站上的照片和尺码表。

#### How much money do you usually spend on shoes?
>I’d say not too much. Normally, I spend around 300 to 500 yuan on a pair. For me, comfort matters more than brand names, so I don’t go for luxury shoes.
我觉得不算多。通常一双鞋我会花三百到五百块。对我来说，舒适度比牌子更重要，所以我不会去买奢侈品牌的鞋。

>Quite a lot. I sometimes spend over 1,000 yuan on a pair, especially if it’s a limited edition sneaker. I see it not just as footwear but also as a kind of collection.
其实挺多的。有时候我会花一千多买一双，特别是限量款的运动鞋。我觉得它不仅是鞋子，也是收藏的一部分。

#### Which do you prefer, fashionable shoes or comfortable shoes?
>I’d definitely go for comfortable shoes. I usually walk a lot during the day, so if the shoes hurt my feet, it doesn’t matter how stylish they look. Comfort always comes first for me.
我肯定更倾向于舒服的鞋。因为我平时走路挺多的，如果鞋子磨脚，再好看也没用。对我来说，舒适永远是第一位的。

>Well, I prefer fashionable shoes. I think shoes can show a person’s style, and wearing trendy ones makes me feel more confident. As long as they’re not too uncomfortable, I don’t mind sacrificing a bit of comfort.
其实我更喜欢时尚的鞋。我觉得鞋子能体现一个人的风格，穿上潮流款会让我更自信。只要不是特别不舒服，我不介意牺牲一点舒适度。

### 23、Doing something well

#### Do you have an experience that you did something well?
>Sure. I remember once I gave a presentation in my university English class. I spent a lot of time preparing slides and practicing. In the end, it went really smoothly, and my teacher praised me in front of everyone.
有的。我记得有一次在大学英语课上做演讲。我花了很多时间准备幻灯片和练习。最后演讲进行得很顺利，老师还在全班同学面前表扬了我。

>Yes. I once cooked a big meal for my family during Spring Festival. I followed some online recipes and managed to make several dishes. Everyone said it tasted great, which made me feel really proud.
有过。春节的时候，我给家人做过一顿大餐。我照着网上的菜谱学做了好几道菜。大家都说味道很好，这让我挺自豪的。

#### Do you have an experience that your teacher thought you did a good job?
>Yes, I do. Once in high school, my Chinese teacher asked me to read an essay I wrote to the whole class. She said it was well-written and clear, which really boosted my confidence.
有的。有一次在高中，语文老师让我把自己写的作文读给全班听。她说写得很好、很清晰，这真的让我自信心大增。

>Actually, yes. During a group project in university, I was the one who gave the final presentation. My professor told me afterwards that I did a great job leading the team and explaining our ideas clearly.
有过。在大学一次小组项目中，我负责最后的展示。教授后来告诉我，我在带领团队和清楚讲解我们想法方面表现得很好。

#### Do you often tell your friends when they do something well?
>Of course. I often tell my friends when they do something well, like giving a good speech or cooking a tasty dish. I think compliments make people feel encouraged, and it also strengthens our friendship.
当然会。我经常在朋友表现好的时候夸他们，比如演讲得好或者菜做得好。我觉得赞美能让人受到鼓励，也能让我们的友谊更牢固。

>Not particularly. I don’t always tell my friends directly, even if they do something well. Sometimes I just smile or show support in other ways, because I’m not very good at expressing my feelings in words.
不太会。就算朋友表现得很好，我也不总是直接说出来。有时候只是笑一下，或者用别的方式表示支持，因为我不太擅长用语言表达感受。

### 24、Crowded place

#### ls the city where you live crowded?
>Definitely. The city where I live is quite crowded, especially during rush hours. On the subway or in shopping malls, you often see huge crowds of people. Sometimes it feels a bit stressful, but I’ve already gotten used to it.
确实是。我住的城市挺拥挤的，尤其是高峰期。在地铁上或者商场里，经常能看到很多人。有时候会有点压力，但我已经习惯了。

>Not particularly. Compared with big cities like Beijing or Shanghai, the place where I live is less crowded. Of course, there are busy areas, but most of the time it’s still easy to find some space.
并不是特别拥挤。和北京、上海这样的特大城市比起来，我住的地方人要少一些。当然也有繁忙的区域，但大多数时候还是能找到自己的空间。

#### ls there a crowded place near where you live?
>Yes. Near my home, there’s a big shopping mall, and it’s always packed on weekends. You can see long lines at restaurants and so many people walking around. It’s lively, but sometimes a bit noisy.
有的。在我家附近有个大型商场，周末总是挤满了人。餐馆门口经常排长队，到处都是人走来走去。氛围很热闹，但有时候有点吵。

>Actually, not really. I live in a quieter neighborhood, so there aren’t many crowded places nearby. If I want to see big crowds, I usually need to go downtown or to tourist spots.
其实没有。我住在比较安静的小区附近，所以周围没什么特别拥挤的地方。如果想看到很多人，我一般得去市中心或者景点。

#### Do you like crowded places?
>Yes. I enjoy crowded places sometimes, like concerts or festivals. The lively atmosphere and energy from the crowd make me feel excited and more connected with others.
其实喜欢。有时候我挺享受人多的地方，比如演唱会或节日活动。那种热闹的氛围和人群的能量会让我觉得兴奋，也更有参与感。

>Honestly, not really. I don’t enjoy crowded places, because they make me feel stressed and tired. I’d rather go somewhere quiet, like a park or a café, where I can relax.
老实说，不太喜欢。我不喜欢人多的地方，因为那让我觉得有压力、很疲惫。我更愿意去安静的地方，比如公园或咖啡馆，在那里放松。

#### Do most people like crowded places?
>I think many people do. Crowded places like shopping malls or festivals can be fun, because people enjoy the lively atmosphere. Being around others makes them feel less lonely and more involved.
我觉得很多人喜欢。像商场或节日活动这样的拥挤场所很有趣，因为人们喜欢那种热闹的氛围。身边有人会让他们不那么孤单，也更有参与感。

>I don’t think so. Many people feel uncomfortable in crowded places, because it’s noisy and tiring. They may enjoy social events sometimes, but most of the time they prefer somewhere quiet and less stressful.
其实我不这么认为。很多人在拥挤的地方会觉得不舒服，因为又吵又累。他们可能偶尔喜欢社交活动，但大多数时候更愿意去安静、不那么有压力的地方。

#### When was the last time you were in a crowded place?
>The last time was just last weekend. I went to a big shopping mall with my friends, and it was packed with people. We had to wait in line for almost half an hour just to get a table at a restaurant.
上一次就是上周末。我和朋友去了一个大型商场，人特别多。我们光是排队等餐馆的座位就花了差不多半个小时。

>I’d say about two months ago. I went to a concert, and the stadium was full of people. The crowd was huge, but the atmosphere was amazing, so I didn’t really mind the noise.
大概是两个月前。我去看了一场演唱会，整个体育场坐满了人。人群非常庞大，但氛围特别好，所以我并不介意噪音。

### 25、Going out

#### Do you bring food or snacks with you when going out?
>Yes. I often bring snacks like fruit or nuts when I go out, especially if I’m going to be out for a long time. It helps me stay energized and avoid getting too hungry.
是的，我会。尤其是要外出很长时间时，我常常带一些水果或坚果。这样可以保持精力，不至于饿得太厉害。

>Not really. I don’t usually bring food or snacks with me when I go out, unless I’m traveling or going on a long trip. I prefer to buy something when I’m out, so I can try different foods.
不太会。我平时外出时不会带食物或零食，除非是旅行或者长途出行。我更喜欢在外面买点东西吃，这样可以尝试不同的食物。

#### Do you always take your mobile phone with you when going out?
>Of course. I always take my phone with me when I go out, because it’s not only for communication but also for maps, mobile payment, and even taking photos. Honestly, without my phone, I’d feel a bit lost.
当然会。我出门总是带着手机，因为它不仅能用来联系，还能导航、移动支付，甚至拍照。老实说，没有手机我会有点不知所措。

>Actually, almost always. If I’m just going downstairs for a quick walk or some exercise, I might leave my phone at home. But if I plan to buy anything, I definitely take it with me, since mobile payment is necessary.
其实几乎总会带。如果只是下楼散步或运动，我可能会把手机放在家里。但如果要买东西，我一定会带，因为移动支付是必须的。

#### Do you often bring cash with you?
>Well,not much. I hardly carry cash these days, because mobile payment is so convenient. I can pay with my phone almost everywhere, from supermarkets to small street vendors.
其实不怎么带。我现在几乎不带现金，因为移动支付太方便了。从超市到小摊贩，几乎到处都能用手机支付。

>Sometimes, yes. I usually bring a small amount of cash, just in case I go somewhere that doesn’t accept mobile payment. It makes me feel more secure to have a backup.
有时候会。我通常会带一点现金，以防去的地方不能用移动支付。随身带着备用的钱让我觉得更安心。

#### How often do you use cash?
>Hardly ever. I almost always use mobile payment in daily life, whether it’s in supermarkets, restaurants, or even street markets. I only use cash when I take a taxi in a small town or when the network doesn’t work.
几乎不会。我在日常生活中几乎都是用移动支付，不管是在超市、餐馆，还是街边小摊。只有在小城打车或者网络不好时，我才会用现金。

>From time to time. I still carry a small amount of cash, just in case my phone runs out of battery. Most of the time I don’t use it, but it makes me feel secure to have some as a backup.
偶尔会。我还是会带一点现金，以防手机没电。大多数时候用不上，但有些备用的钱让我觉得更安心。

### 26、Staying with old people

#### Have you ever worked with old people?
>Yes, I have. During a community volunteer activity, I worked with some elderly people to organize a charity event. They were really patient and experienced, and I learned a lot from them.
有的。在一次社区志愿活动中，我和一些老人一起组织过慈善活动。他们特别有耐心，也很有经验，我从他们身上学到了不少东西。

>Actually, no. I haven’t really worked with old people before. Most of my teamwork has been with classmates or colleagues around my age. But I think it would be interesting, because older people often have different perspectives.
